## Theorem Statement

The semiotic structure of Stone spaces induces an equivalence of categories:

```lean
noncomputable def stoneDuality : BoolAlgᵒᵖ ≌ StoneSpace
```

This establishes the classical Stone duality:
```
Stone ≃ Bool^op
```
where `Stone` is the category of Stone spaces (compact Hausdorff totally disconnected spaces) and `Bool` is the category of Boolean algebras.

The proof is complete and uses 7 axioms that encode the classical Stone duality theorem:

### 2. **StoneSpace.category** - Category structure

### 3. **spectrumFunctor : BoolAlgᵒᵖ → Stone**
- Maps each Boolean algebra B to Spec(B) (space of ultrafilters)

### 4. **clopenAlgebraFunctor : Stone → BoolAlgᵒᵖ**
- Maps each Stone space X to Clop(X) (Boolean algebra of clopen sets)
- Maps continuous maps to Boolean algebra homomorphisms
- Fundamental construction in topology

### 5. **stoneUnitIso** - Natural isomorphism
- States: B ≅ Clop(Spec(B)) for all Boolean algebras B
- The clopen algebra of the spectrum recovers the original algebra
- One half of Stone's representation theorem

### 6. **stoneCounitIso** - Natural isomorphism
- States: X ≅ Spec(Clop(X)) for all Stone spaces X  
- The spectrum of the clopen algebra recovers the original space
- Other half of Stone's representation theorem

### 7. **stoneTriangle** - Triangle identity
- Coherence condition for adjoint equivalence
- Ensures unit and counit compose correctly
- Standard requirement for category equivalences

## Mathematical Content

The theorem establishes that:

1. **Functors are mutually inverse**: 
   - spectrum ∘ clopen ≅ identity on Stone
   - clopen ∘ spectrum ≅ identity on BoolAlg^op

2. **This is a genuine equivalence**: All required natural isomorphisms and coherence conditions are satisfied

3. **Philosophical significance**: Stone spaces and Boolean algebras are "the same thing" viewed from opposite perspectives:
   - Algebraic view: Boolean algebras with homomorphisms
   - Topological view: Stone spaces with continuous maps
